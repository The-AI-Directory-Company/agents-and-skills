# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Effective date:** 2025-01-01
**Last updated:** 2025-01-01
**Applies to:** All full-time employees in the United States
**Supersedes:** PTO Policy v2.1, dated 2023-06-15

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for requesting and using paid time off, ensuring consistent treatment across teams while supporting employee wellbeing. PTO enables employees to rest, manage personal responsibilities, and return to work engaged and productive.

---

## Scope

- **Included:** Full-time employees who have completed their introductory period (first 90 days)
- **Excluded:** Part-time employees (see Part-Time Benefits Policy), independent contractors, interns
- **Geographic exceptions:** Employees in California and New York should review the jurisdiction-specific provisions in the appendix below

---

## Policy Details

### Option A: Accrual-Based PTO

Use this structure when the company uses accrual-based PTO.

**4.1 Accrual Rate**

Employees accrue PTO based on length of service:

| Tenure | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Rate (per pay period, semi-monthly) |
|--------|----------------|---------------------------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 5.0 hours |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.67 hours |
| 6+ years | 25 days (200 hours) | 8.33 hours |

Accrual begins on the first day of employment. PTO is not available for use during the 90-day introductory period but accrues during that time.

**4.2 Maximum Accrual Cap**

Employees may accrue up to 1.5x their annual allotment. Once the cap is reached, accrual pauses until PTO is used. Example: an employee with 15 days/year stops accruing at 22.5 days (180 hours).

**4.3 Carryover**

Unused PTO carries over to the next calendar year, subject to the maximum accrual cap. There is no use-it-or-lose-it deadline.

**4.4 Payout at Separation**

Accrued, unused PTO is paid out upon separation of employment at the employee's regular rate of pay. This applies regardless of the reason for separation.

### Option B: Unlimited PTO

Use this structure when the company uses an unlimited/flexible PTO model.

**4.1 PTO Availability**

Employees may take paid time off as needed, with no fixed annual cap. There is no accrual, no balance to track, and no payout at separation.

**4.2 Minimum Usage Expectation**

To ensure employees actually use this benefit, the company sets a minimum expectation of 15 days off per year. Managers are expected to monitor usage and encourage employees who have not taken time off in 8+ weeks to schedule PTO.

**4.3 Approval and Notice**

All PTO requires manager approval. The minimum usage expectation does not create an entitlement to take time off at any specific time — operational needs may require scheduling adjustments.

## Procedures

### How to Request PTO

1. Submit a PTO request in [HRIS System Name] at least 5 business days before the requested start date
2. For requests of 5+ consecutive days, submit at least 15 business days in advance
3. Your manager will respond within 2 business days of the request
4. You will receive a confirmation email when the request is approved

### How Managers Approve PTO

1. Review the request in [HRIS System Name] within 2 business days
2. Check team coverage — ensure no more than 30% of the team is out on the same dates
3. Approve or discuss alternative dates with the employee
4. If denying, provide a specific reason and suggest alternative dates

### Unplanned Absences

1. Notify your manager before your scheduled start time via [Slack/email/phone]
2. If you cannot reach your manager, contact [backup contact or HR]
3. Submit the PTO request in [HRIS System Name] retroactively on your first day back

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I take a half day?**
A: Yes. PTO can be used in increments of 4 hours (half day). Submit the request in [HRIS System Name] and select "Half Day" as the request type.

**Q: What happens to my PTO if I switch from part-time to full-time?**
A: PTO accrual begins based on your full-time start date. Prior part-time service counts toward tenure for accrual rate purposes but does not carry a PTO balance.

**Q: Are company holidays separate from PTO?**
A: Yes. The company observes [X] paid holidays per year (see Company Holiday Schedule). These do not reduce your PTO balance.

**Q: Can my manager deny my PTO request?**
A: Managers may ask you to adjust dates for operational reasons but should not unreasonably deny PTO. If you believe a denial is unreasonable, contact People Operations.

**Q: What if I get sick during PTO?**
A: Sick leave is separate from PTO (see Sick Leave Policy). If you become ill during a scheduled PTO period, you may convert those days to sick leave by notifying your manager and providing documentation if required.

## Appendix: Jurisdiction-Specific Provisions

### California

- PTO is considered earned wages under California law. Accrued PTO must be paid out at separation. Use-it-or-lose-it policies are prohibited.
- Accrual caps are permitted, but the cap must be reasonable (the California DLSE considers 1.5-2x the annual accrual rate reasonable).
- For unlimited PTO: California courts have not fully resolved whether unlimited PTO requires payout at separation. The company's position is that under a true unlimited policy with no accrual, there is no vested benefit to pay out. Consult legal counsel before implementing.

### New York

- New York does not require PTO payout at separation unless the company's policy or practice creates an expectation of payout.
- New York City requires employers with 5+ employees to provide up to 40 hours of paid safe and sick leave per year. This is separate from PTO.
- Ensure the PTO policy does not conflict with New York's paid sick leave requirements — PTO may satisfy these requirements only if it meets all statutory conditions.
