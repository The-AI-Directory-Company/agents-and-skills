# Jurisdiction Compliance Flags

Employment law flags organized by jurisdiction. Use this reference when writing handbook sections to identify provisions that vary by location and require jurisdiction-specific language.

**Important:** This reference identifies areas requiring legal attention. It is not legal advice. Every handbook section must be reviewed by qualified legal counsel familiar with the applicable jurisdictions before publication.

---

## United States — State-Level Flags

### California

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| PTO/Vacation | Payout required | Accrued vacation is earned wages; must be paid at separation. Use-it-or-lose-it is prohibited. Accrual caps are permitted if reasonable (1.5-2x annual rate). |
| Sick leave | Mandatory | Minimum 5 days (40 hours) paid sick leave per year (as of 2024). Accrual or front-load permitted. Carryover required if accrual-based. |
| Meal/rest breaks | Strict requirements | 30-min unpaid meal break before 5th hour; 10-min paid rest break per 4 hours. Penalties for violations. |
| At-will employment | Limited | At-will doctrine applies but is narrowed by extensive public policy, implied contract, and covenant of good faith exceptions. |
| Non-competes | Prohibited | Non-compete agreements are void and unenforceable (effective 2024, with retroactive application). |
| Pay transparency | Required | Salary ranges must be included in job postings. Pay scale must be provided to current employees upon request. |
| Remote work | Nexus rules | Employees working remotely from CA may create CA employment obligations regardless of employer's home state. |
| Expense reimbursement | Required | Employers must reimburse all necessary business expenses (Labor Code 2802). Includes home office expenses for remote workers. |

### New York

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| PTO/Vacation | Payout depends on policy | No statute requires payout, but company policy or established practice may create an enforceable obligation. |
| Sick leave | Mandatory | Employers with 5+ employees must provide up to 40 hours paid sick leave per year (NYC: 40 hours for 5+ employees; state: scaled by employer size). |
| Meal breaks | Required | 30-min unpaid meal break for shifts over 6 hours. Additional break for shifts spanning certain hours. |
| Non-competes | Restricted | Proposed ban pending; currently enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography. Expect changes. |
| Pay transparency | Required (NYC) | NYC employers must include salary range in job postings and promotions. NY State law also requires it for employers with 4+ employees. |
| Wage theft protections | Strong | NY WARN Act (90-day notice for mass layoffs), Wage Theft Prevention Act (written wage notice at hire). |

### Texas

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| PTO/Vacation | Employer discretion | No state law requires PTO or vacation. If offered, payout at separation is governed by company policy. |
| Sick leave | No state mandate | No statewide paid sick leave requirement. Some cities (Austin, Dallas, San Antonio) have attempted local ordinances — check current enforcement status. |
| At-will employment | Broad | Strong at-will doctrine with limited exceptions. |
| Non-competes | Enforceable if reasonable | Must be ancillary to an otherwise enforceable agreement. Reasonable in time, scope, and geography. |
| Pay transparency | Not required | No salary disclosure mandate. |

### Colorado

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Pay transparency | Required | Salary range must be in all job postings, including remote roles accessible to CO residents. |
| Sick leave | Mandatory | Minimum 48 hours paid sick leave per year. |
| Non-competes | Highly restricted | Non-competes prohibited for most workers earning under a threshold (~$112K as of 2024). Narrow exceptions for management and trade secrets. |
| Overtime | Expanded | CO overtime rules may differ from FLSA; check Colorado Overtime and Minimum Pay Standards (COMPS) order. |

### Washington

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Sick leave | Mandatory | 1 hour per 40 hours worked. No waiting period. |
| Non-competes | Restricted | Enforceable only for employees earning above ~$116K/year (adjusted annually). Requires garden leave pay. |
| Pay transparency | Required | Salary range in job postings for employers with 15+ employees. |
| Long-term care | Mandatory payroll tax | WA Cares Fund: mandatory payroll tax for long-term care (employees may opt out under limited conditions). |

### Illinois

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| PTO/Vacation | Payout required | Accrued vacation must be paid at separation (IL Wage Payment and Collection Act). |
| Sick leave | Mandatory | Paid Leave for All Workers Act: 40 hours paid leave per year for any reason. |
| Non-competes | Restricted | Unenforceable for employees earning under $75K/year. Requires consideration and 14-day review period. |

## International — Key Jurisdiction Flags

### European Union / EEA

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Minimum annual leave | 4 weeks mandatory | EU Working Time Directive: minimum 20 working days paid annual leave. Many member states exceed this (e.g., France: 25 days + RTT). Cannot be waived or bought out. |
| Termination | Cause required in most jurisdictions | At-will employment does not exist. Termination requires valid cause, notice periods (often 1-3 months), and in some countries, works council consultation. |
| Probation periods | Regulated | Maximum probation periods vary (e.g., Germany: 6 months; France: 2-4 months renewable). |
| Working hours | Capped | Maximum 48 hours/week averaged over a reference period. Some countries have stricter limits (e.g., France: 35-hour workweek). |
| Data privacy | GDPR applies to employee data | Employee monitoring, performance tracking, and HR data processing require GDPR compliance, including lawful basis and data minimization. |
| Non-competes | Compensation required | Many EU jurisdictions (Germany, France, Italy) require compensating employees during non-compete periods (typically 50-100% of salary). |

### United Kingdom

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Annual leave | 28 days minimum | 5.6 weeks statutory annual leave (28 days for full-time), inclusive of bank holidays. |
| Notice periods | Statutory minimum | 1 week per year of service, up to 12 weeks. Contracts may specify longer periods. |
| Redundancy | Consultation required | Collective consultation required for 20+ redundancies. Statutory redundancy pay based on tenure. |
| Non-competes | Under reform | Currently enforceable if reasonable. UK government has proposed limiting non-competes to 3 months. |

### Canada

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Provincial variation | Significant | Employment standards are primarily provincial. Each province has different rules for vacation, sick leave, overtime, and termination. |
| Termination | Notice or pay in lieu | Common law reasonable notice (often far exceeding statutory minimums) applies unless explicitly contracted out. |
| Annual leave | Provincial | Ranges from 2 weeks (most provinces, first 5 years) to 3+ weeks with tenure. |
| Sick leave | Provincial | Varies: some provinces mandate paid sick days, others only unpaid protected leave. |

### Australia

| Topic | Flag | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Annual leave | 4 weeks minimum | National Employment Standards: 20 days paid annual leave per year, cumulative with no cap. |
| Sick/personal leave | 10 days per year | Paid personal/carer's leave, cumulative. |
| Unfair dismissal | Protected | Employees with 6+ months tenure (12 months for small businesses) can claim unfair dismissal. Must follow proper process. |
| Modern Awards | Industry-specific minimums | Most employees are covered by a Modern Award setting minimum pay, conditions, and entitlements by industry/occupation. |

## Handbook Section Checklist — Jurisdiction Awareness

When writing any handbook section, verify:

- [ ] Identified all jurisdictions where employees are located
- [ ] Checked state/provincial requirements for the specific policy topic
- [ ] Flagged jurisdiction-specific provisions with explicit callouts (e.g., "Employees in California: see Section X")
- [ ] Verified the policy meets or exceeds the most restrictive applicable jurisdiction
- [ ] Included a legal review disclaimer
- [ ] Noted any pending legislation that may affect the policy
