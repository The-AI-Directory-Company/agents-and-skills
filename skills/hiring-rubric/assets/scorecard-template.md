# Scorecard Template

A blank, role-agnostic scorecard for interviewers to complete within 24 hours of the interview. Copy and fill in the role-specific dimensions before distributing to the panel.

## Instructions

1. Replace the placeholder dimensions with the 4-6 dimensions from the role's hiring rubric.
2. Distribute one copy per interviewer per round.
3. Interviewers must complete the Evidence column for every score — a score without a specific observation is not valid.
4. Submit before the debrief meeting. Do not discuss scores with other interviewers before submitting.

## Scoring Guide

Use this scale consistently across all dimensions:

| Score | Label       | Meaning |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 4     | Strong Hire | Exceeds expectations for this level. Proactively demonstrates depth without prompting. |
| 3     | Hire        | Meets expectations for this level. Demonstrates competence with minor gaps. |
| 2     | Weak        | Below expectations. Requires significant guidance or misses key aspects. |
| 1     | No Hire     | Does not meet the bar. Fundamental gaps for this level. |

## Rules

- **Evidence is mandatory.** Write the specific moment, quote, or behavior that justified each score. "Seemed strong" is not evidence. "Identified the race condition in the design without prompting and proposed a locking strategy with tradeoff analysis" is evidence.
- **Score independently.** Do not discuss your scores with other interviewers before the debrief.
- **Submit within 24 hours.** Memory degrades quickly. Complete the scorecard as soon as possible after the interview.
- **Use the full scale.** If every candidate gets a 3, the rubric is not doing its job. A 4 should be rare and a 1 should be clearly justified.
