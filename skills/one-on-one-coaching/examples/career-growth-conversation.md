# 1:1 Example: Career Growth Conversation

## Context

**Direct report:** Nadia Okafor, Senior Frontend Engineer, 18 months on the team
**1:1 type:** Career growth + feedback delivery
**Since last 1:1:** Nadia led the checkout redesign launch (shipped on time, 12% conversion lift). She also missed the deadline on the design system documentation she volunteered for.

## Preparation (5 minutes before)

1. **Last 1:1 follow-up:** Nadia committed to drafting the design system color token docs by March 7. Status: not started. I committed to connecting her with the Staff Engineer on the platform team for a mentorship chat. Status: done, meeting happened March 4.
2. **Topics I want to raise:** (a) Recognize the checkout launch win, (b) Discuss the missed documentation deadline, (c) Career growth — she mentioned interest in the Staff Engineer track last month.
3. **Single most important question:** "What does Nadia think is the gap between her current work and Staff-level impact?"

## Opening

**Me:** "What's been on your mind this week?"

**Nadia:** Talked about post-launch fatigue from the checkout project, mentioned feeling pulled between feature work and the design system documentation.

*(Did not redirect to status — she's raising a real tension. Follow the thread.)*

## Feedback: Positive (SBI)

```
Situation: During the checkout redesign launch last Tuesday
Behavior:  You coordinated the rollout across 3 teams, caught the payment
           flow regression in staging, and made the call to delay by one day
           rather than ship with the bug
Impact:    We launched cleanly with zero post-deploy incidents. The PM told
           me it was the smoothest cross-team launch she's been part of.
           That's Staff-level judgment.
```

**Nadia's response:** Appreciated the recognition. Said the hardest part was pushing back on the PM's original timeline — she wasn't sure she had the authority to delay.

**My follow-up:** "You absolutely had the authority, and you exercised it well. That's exactly the kind of ownership we expect at Staff level."

## Feedback: Constructive (SBI)

```
Situation: The design system color token documentation, due March 7
Behavior:  You haven't started the draft, and you didn't flag that it was
           slipping until I asked today
Impact:    The platform team is blocked on publishing the v2 tokens because
           they're waiting on your section. They've had to answer questions
           manually that the docs would have covered.
```

**Me:** "What's your perspective on this?"

**Nadia:** Acknowledged she over-committed. The checkout project consumed all her time, and she didn't want to seem like she couldn't handle both.

**My response:** "I'd rather hear 'I need to push this back' a week early than discover it slipped. Can you get the draft done by March 21? And in the future, flag scope conflicts in our 1:1 before they become blockers."

**Agreed action:** Nadia will deliver the draft by March 21 and proactively flag competing commitments going forward.

## Career Growth Discussion

**Me:** "You mentioned wanting to work toward Staff Engineer. Where do you think the biggest gap is between where you are now and that level?"

**Nadia:** Thinks her technical skills are strong but she struggles with "influencing without authority" — getting other teams to adopt her proposals.

**Discussion:** We identified a concrete gap: Nadia writes solid technical proposals but presents them only in written form. The engineers who get cross-team buy-in at Staff level present in person, run working sessions, and build consensus before the formal proposal.

**30-day action:** Nadia will lead one cross-team working session for the design system token migration. I will pair her with Raj (Staff Engineer) to observe how he runs his API working sessions before she facilitates her own.

## Closing

**Commitments:**
1. Nadia: Design system color token docs draft by March 21.
2. Nadia: Lead one cross-team working session by April 15.
3. Me: Introduce Nadia to Raj by end of this week for working session shadowing.
4. Both: Revisit Staff Engineer gap assessment in next month's career-focused 1:1.

**Me:** "Anything we didn't cover?"

**Nadia:** Asked about the Q2 project allocations — wants to work on the notification preferences feature. Noted for sprint planning discussion with PM.
