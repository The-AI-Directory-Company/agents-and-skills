# Product Launch Brief Template

Use this blank template for any launch tier. For Tier 3 launches, sections 6 and 8 can be simplified — but do not skip them entirely.

---

- **Launch Owner:** [Full name, role]

## 2. Positioning

**For** [target audience] **who** [current situation or pain point],
**this** [product/feature] **provides** [key benefit],
**unlike** [current alternative or competitor], **it** [key differentiator].

### Messaging Versions

**Website version:**
[1-2 sentences for the landing page or feature page. Customer-facing, benefit-led.]

**Sales conversation version:**
[2-3 sentences a rep can use on a call. Includes the pain, benefit, and a proof point.]

[2-3 sentences for the internal team. Includes strategic context and what this replaces or builds on.]

| Date | Milestone | Owner |

| L-21 | [Sales enablement materials ready] | [Full name, role] |
| L-14 | [Blog post, landing page, video in review] | [Full name, role] |
| L-7 | [Sales trained, press briefings begin, App Store updated] | [Full name, role] |
| L-3 | [In-app announcements configured, support FAQ published] | [Full name, role] |

---

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Positioning answers "for whom, what benefit, versus what alternative" in one statement
- [ ] Target audience includes current behavior and desired behavior
- [ ] Key messages limited to 3-5 with proof points and objection handling
- [ ] Timeline works backward from launch date with realistic lead times
- [ ] Every channel has an owner, publish date, and defined CTA
- [ ] Success metrics have numeric targets, underperformance thresholds, and measurement methods
- [ ] Every cross-functional deliverable has a single named owner and due date
- [ ] The brief fits on 2-3 pages when filled in
