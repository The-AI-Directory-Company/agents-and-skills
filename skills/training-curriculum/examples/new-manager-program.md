# Training Curriculum: New Engineering Manager Program

## Curriculum Overview

- **Target outcome**: After completing this program, new engineering managers can run effective 1:1s, deliver actionable feedback, plan team capacity, manage performance, and navigate their first hiring cycle.
- **Audience**: Individual contributors promoted to engineering manager within the last 6 months. Prerequisite: 2+ years as a senior engineer at the company.
- **Format**: Cohort-based, instructor-led with async pre-work. Cohorts of 8-12 managers.
- **Duration**: 20 hours over 8 weeks (2.5 hours/week: 30 min pre-work + 2 hours session).

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this program, participants will be able to:

1. **Structure a 1:1** that surfaces blockers, supports career growth, and builds trust (Apply)
2. **Deliver feedback** using the Situation-Behavior-Impact model within 48 hours of an observed event (Apply)
3. **Build a team capacity plan** that accounts for sprint commitments, on-call, and growth work (Create)
4. **Identify and address** underperformance through a documented improvement plan (Analyze)
5. **Design a hiring rubric** with leveled competencies and structured interview questions (Create)
6. **Facilitate a sprint retrospective** that produces actionable commitments, not just discussion (Apply)
7. **Assess and communicate** technical trade-offs to non-technical stakeholders (Evaluate)
8. **Recognize and mitigate** the three most common first-time manager failure modes (Analyze)

## Module Detail: Module 3 — Giving and Receiving Feedback

- **Pre-work** (30 min): Read "Radical Candor" Chapter 2. Write down one piece of feedback you have been delaying.
- **Content outline**: SBI model explained, timing principles (48-hour rule), common avoidance patterns, receiving feedback without defensiveness, feedback in remote/async settings.
- **Exercise**: Paired role-play. Each participant delivers the feedback they identified in pre-work to a partner playing the recipient. Partner scores delivery on clarity, specificity, and actionability using a provided rubric.
- **Key takeaways**: Feedback delayed is feedback devalued. Use SBI to separate observation from judgment. Ask "what would be most helpful?" before giving unsolicited advice.

## Exercises

```
Exercise: Build Your Team's Capacity Plan
Scenario: Your team of 6 engineers has sprint commitments, 1 person on-call rotation,
          a planned migration, and a request for a new feature from the VP of Product.
Task:     Using the provided spreadsheet template, allocate team capacity across
          commitments for the next 4 weeks. Identify what must be deferred and draft
          a one-paragraph justification for the trade-off to share with your skip-level.
Expected: Completed capacity grid with no over-allocation, written trade-off rationale.
Time:     40 minutes
```

## Assessment Methods

| Assessment | When | What It Measures | Pass Criteria |
|-----------|------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1:1 simulation | End of Module 2 | Can structure and lead a productive 1:1 | Peer + facilitator score 3/5 or higher on rubric |
| Feedback role-play | End of Module 3 | Can deliver clear, timely feedback using SBI | Partner rates delivery as "actionable" on all 3 dimensions |
| Capacity plan exercise | End of Module 4 | Can build a realistic team plan with trade-offs | No over-allocation; trade-off rationale is coherent |
| Hiring rubric review | End of Module 6 | Can design a leveled, bias-resistant rubric | Rubric covers 4+ competencies with observable indicators at each level |
| 90-day plan presentation | Module 8 (capstone) | Synthesis of all objectives into a personal plan | Peer cohort + program lead score 3/5 on rubric |

## Progression Milestones

- **After Module 2**: "I can run a 1:1 that my report finds useful, without relying on a status update format."
- **After Module 5**: "I can identify a performance gap, have the conversation, and document next steps."
- **After Module 8**: "I have a written 90-day plan for my team and have presented it to peers for feedback."
