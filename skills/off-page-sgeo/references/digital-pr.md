# Digital PR

Getting featured in publications, podcasts, and roundups builds both traditional authority (backlinks, brand mentions) and AI citation surface area. Journalists produce content that AI engines treat as authoritative sources. Every press mention is simultaneously a backlink opportunity, a brand mention, and an AI citation pathway.

If your pitch doesn't have a number in the subject line, it's going to the bottom of the inbox.

---

## Journalist Identification

Before you pitch, build a list of people who actually cover your topic. Cold-pitching the wrong journalist wastes both of your time.

### Finding Relevant Journalists

1. **Search by beat:** Look for reporters who cover your niche. Search "[topic] reporter" or "[topic] journalist" on Twitter/X. Check bylines on the publications you want to appear in.
2. **Use Muck Rack** (paid, ~$5K/year): The industry-standard journalist database. Search by topic, publication, and recent coverage. Shows journalist contact info and recent articles. Worth it if PR is a core channel.
3. **Manual research** (free): For each target publication, check the staff/contributors page. Find 2-3 writers who cover your topic area. Read their recent articles to understand their angle and interests.
4. **Follow journalist query platforms (Qwoted, Featured.com, Terkel):** Journalists post specific questions they need expert sources for. Subscribe to the daily digest. Filter for your topic area. Respond within 2 hours — late responses rarely get used. (HARO and Connectively, previously the dominant services here, were discontinued in 2023-2024.)

### Building Your List

Create a list of 20-30 relevant journalists:

| Journalist | Publication | Beat | Recent Article | Contact | Relationship Status |
|-----------|-------------|------|----------------|---------|---------------------|
| | | | | | Not contacted / Engaged / Pitched |

Before pitching anyone:
- Follow them on Twitter/X
- Read their last 5 articles
- Comment on or share at least one piece meaningfully
- Build recognition before asking for coverage

This pre-pitch engagement takes 2-3 weeks but dramatically improves response rates.

## Pitch Construction: The Data-Lead Format

Journalists get hundreds of pitches. Yours needs to earn attention in 5 seconds. Lead with a specific, surprising number.

### Anatomy of an Effective Pitch

```
Subject: [Most Surprising Statistic or Angle] — for [Publication Name]

Hi [First Name],

[One sentence connecting to something they specifically cover — reference
a recent article they wrote. Show you read their work.]

[Two sentences with the data or story, leading with a specific number.
Include one statistic that makes someone stop scrolling.]

[One sentence on why you or your company is credible on this topic —
role, years of experience, dataset size, or unique access.]

Happy to share the full [report/dataset/analysis]. Would you like to take
a look?

[Your Name]
[Title, Company]
```

### Three Pitch Examples

**Example 1: Data Study Pitch**

```
Subject: 62% of SaaS companies block AI crawlers accidentally — TechCrunch

Hi Sarah,

Your recent piece on AI search disruption resonated with something we've
been researching.

We analyzed 10,000 SaaS company websites and found that 62% accidentally
block AI crawlers through default CDN settings — most don't know they're
invisible to ChatGPT and Perplexity. The companies that fixed this saw
AI-referred traffic increase 3-5x within 60 days.

I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. We've been tracking AI crawler access
patterns across 50K+ domains for the past 18 months.

Happy to share the full dataset and methodology. Interested?

[Name]
[Title, Company]
```

**Example 2: Expert Commentary Pitch**

```
Subject: Source: AI search impact on B2B buying — Forbes

Hi Michael,

I saw your Forbes column on B2B marketing trends — the section on buyer
research behavior matched patterns we're seeing in our data.

We track how B2B buyers use AI assistants during purchase research, and
the latest data shows 34% of B2B buyers now consult ChatGPT or Perplexity
before contacting a vendor — up from 8% a year ago. This is reshaping
how companies need to think about visibility.

I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. I've been in B2B marketing for 12 years
and have been focused specifically on AI search since 2024.

Available for a quote or a deeper conversation whenever works.

[Name]
[Title, Company]
```

**Example 3: Trend Analysis Pitch**

```
Subject: AI-referred traffic up 527% YoY — how brands adapt — Search Engine Land

Hi [Name],

Following up on your Search Engine Land coverage of AI Overviews — we've
been tracking the traffic side of this story.

Our analysis of 500 websites shows AI-referred sessions grew 527% year-
over-year in the first five months of 2025, but only 12% of marketing
teams track this referral source. The brands that do track it are adjusting
content strategy specifically for AI citation.

I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. We've been publishing data on AI search
impact since early 2024.

I can share the dataset or provide commentary. Which would be more useful?

[Name]
[Title, Company]
```

## Timing and Follow-Up

- **Best days to pitch:** Tuesday through Thursday mornings (journalist's local time)
- **Worst days:** Monday (inbox overload) and Friday (winding down for weekend)
- **Follow up once** after 3-5 business days if no response
- **Never follow up more than twice total.** If they don't respond after two emails, they're not interested. Move on.
- **Journalist query platforms (Qwoted, Featured.com, Terkel):** Respond within 2 hours of the query posting. Include 1-2 data points and your credentials. Late responses rarely get used. HARO and Connectively were discontinued in 2023-2024.

### Response Rate Expectations

| Channel | Response Rate | Conversion to Coverage |
|---------|--------------|----------------------|
| Cold pitch (no prior engagement) | 5-10% | 2-5% |
| Warm contact (pre-pitch engagement) | 15-25% | 8-12% |
| Journalist query platforms — Qwoted, Featured.com, Terkel (within 2 hours) | 15-25% | 10-15% |
| Podcast pitch | 10-20% | 5-10% |

These numbers mean you need volume. To get 2-3 press mentions per month, you need 30-50 active outreach threads.

## Podcast Guesting

Podcasts are an underrated authority channel. Show notes generate permanent backlinks. Transcripts get indexed by search engines and AI. And unlike written content, podcast appearances position you as a person with expertise — AI engines associate that with your brand.

### Finding Relevant Podcasts

- Search "[your niche] podcast" on Apple Podcasts and Spotify
- Check episode topics and guest quality — if the show features your competitors or peers, it's a fit
- Look for shows with 50+ episodes (established, committed) and active social media presence
- Check if they publish show notes with guest links (this is where your backlink comes from)

### Podcast Pitch Template

```
Subject: Guest pitch — [Your Topic Angle]

Hi [Host Name],

I've been listening to [Podcast Name] — your episode with [recent guest]
on [topic] was particularly interesting because [specific observation].

I'd love to come on and discuss [specific angle]. Here are three topic
ideas your audience might find valuable:

1. [Topic 1 — with a hook: "Why X is happening and what it means for Y"]
2. [Topic 2 — with data: "Our data shows X — and most people get it wrong"]
3. [Topic 3 — contrarian take: "The conventional wisdom on X is backwards"]

About me: [2-sentence bio with credentials and relevant experience].
[Link to a previous podcast appearance or talk, if available.]

Happy to adapt any of these to fit your show's style.

[Your Name]
```

### Show Notes Optimization

- Provide the host with a keyword-rich bio and topic description
- Ensure they link to your site in the show notes (specify the URL)
- Suggest chapter markers/timestamps for key discussion points
- Share the episode on your platforms — hosts appreciate amplification

## Awards, Lists, and Speaking

These generate high-authority mentions and links with relatively low ongoing effort.

### Awards and "Best Of" Lists

- Search for "[your industry] awards" and "[your category] best of [year]" to find nomination opportunities
- Industry awards (SaaS awards, product awards, marketing awards) generate press coverage when you win or are shortlisted
- "Top 50" roundups and "Best tools for X" lists are high-citation content that AI engines frequently reference
- Submit to relevant awards quarterly — the investment is the application time

### Conference Speaking

- Speaker pages on conference websites link to your site with professional context
- Recorded talks become permanently searchable content on YouTube or conference platforms
- Slides shared on SlideShare or your site become linkable assets
- Conference networking generates warm contacts for future PR pitches

Target 2-4 speaking engagements per year at conferences your audience attends. Each generates a speaker page link, a potential video, social media coverage, and networking opportunities.

## Measuring PR Impact

Track PR efforts against both SEO and GEO outcomes:

| Metric | What to Track | Cadence |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Press mentions | Total coverage pieces, publication authority | Monthly |
| Backlinks from coverage | New referring domains from PR placements | Monthly |
| Brand mention growth | Increase in brand mentions across web | Monthly |
| AI citation change | Before/after AI visibility for covered topics | Monthly |
| Referral traffic | Traffic from press placements | Weekly |

The best PR efforts compound: a press mention leads to a Wikipedia-notable source, which leads to a Wikipedia article, which leads to high-confidence AI citation. Think in terms of cascading authority, not isolated placements.
