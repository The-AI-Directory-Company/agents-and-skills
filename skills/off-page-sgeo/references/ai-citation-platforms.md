# AI Citation Platforms

AI engines cite sources beyond your website. If your brand only exists on your domain, you limit citation potential to a single source. Building presence on platforms that AI engines frequently retrieve from expands your citation surface area across both search and AI discovery channels.

This is the key GEO differentiator in off-page strategy. Traditional SEO off-page work focuses on backlinks to your domain. SGEO off-page work additionally builds your brand's presence on the platforms AI engines most frequently cite.

The seven platforms below are ordered by observed AI citation frequency. Each section covers why the platform matters, how to build presence, what to do, what to avoid, and what high-citation content patterns look like.

---

## 1. Reddit

Reddit is the single most important third-party platform for AI citation. Both ChatGPT and Perplexity heavily index Reddit content. When users ask AI for recommendations, opinions, or comparisons, Reddit threads are among the first sources cited because AI engines treat them as authentic user perspectives.

### Why AI Cites Reddit

- AI engines value authentic opinions over marketing copy. Reddit's community moderation means low-quality content gets downvoted and hidden.
- Reddit threads match the conversational query format AI users employ. "What's the best X for Y?" maps directly to Reddit thread titles.
- Reddit's content is structured as questions and answers — the exact format AI engines are designed to process.
- OpenAI has a content licensing deal with Reddit. Perplexity indexes Reddit aggressively.

### Building Presence

**Subreddit selection:**
1. Identify 3-5 subreddits where your target audience asks questions about your domain
2. Check subscriber count (>10K for meaningful reach), post frequency (>5 posts/day for active communities), and moderator rules
3. Read the subreddit rules carefully — many have strict anti-promotion policies
4. Use Reddit search to find where your brand or competitors are already discussed

**Account building:**
- Use your real identity or a clearly professional username (not "BrandMarketer2026")
- Build karma and post history over 2-4 weeks before any brand-related contributions
- Participate genuinely: answer questions, share expertise, upvote and comment on others' posts
- Your post history is visible. Moderators and community members check it.

### Do's

- Answer questions with genuine expertise — 200+ word answers with specific details, numbers, and personal experience
- Share relevant resources (including yours) only when they directly answer someone's question
- Provide specific comparisons between tools/approaches when asked, including honest pros and cons
- Engage in discussions as a domain expert, not as a brand representative
- Be transparent about affiliations if directly asked

### Don'ts

- Never create promotional posts disguised as questions ("Has anyone tried [your product]? I just discovered it and...")
- Never use multiple accounts to upvote your own content or post fake testimonials
- Never have employees brigade threads with coordinated posts
- Never open with "I'm the founder of X and..." — this signals marketing, not contribution
- Never post links without substantial accompanying text. A link-only comment is spam.

### High-Citation Content Patterns

The Reddit content that AI engines cite most frequently:
- **Detailed experience reports:** "I used X for 6 months, here's what happened" with specific metrics
- **Head-to-head comparisons:** "I tested both X and Y for [specific use case], here's the breakdown" with pros/cons
- **Data-backed recommendations:** "After analyzing [number] of options, these are the top 3 for [specific need]"
- **Expert explanations:** Thorough answers to "how does X work" or "why does X matter" questions

## 2. YouTube

YouTube is the second-largest search engine and a growing AI citation source. AI engines index video transcripts and descriptions, making spoken content discoverable and citable. When AI cannot watch a video, it reads the auto-transcript and the description — both are citation-eligible.

### Why AI Cites YouTube

- Transcripts provide dense, topic-specific content in natural language
- YouTube videos rank in Google search results, making them double-indexed
- Video content demonstrates expertise through teaching, which AI values for E-E-A-T signals
- Long-form videos (10+ minutes) generate substantial transcript content that covers topics comprehensively

### Building Presence

- Create a branded channel with complete About page, links to your site, and consistent branding
- Focus on tutorial and explainer content — "how to" videos are the most frequently cited format
- Publish consistently (weekly or biweekly minimum) to build the channel's authority

### Content Optimization for AI Citation

**Spoken content matters most:**
- Speak clearly using topic-specific terminology. AI extracts from auto-transcripts.
- Say your brand name and key terms naturally during the video. The transcript becomes a text document where these terms appear.
- Structure your spoken content with clear transitions: "First... Second... The key takeaway is..."

**Descriptions (500+ words):**
- AI reads video descriptions even when it cannot process the video itself
- Write detailed descriptions that summarize the video's key points, include relevant keywords, and link to resources mentioned
- Don't just write "In this video I talk about X." Write a genuine summary with the main insights.

**Chapters and timestamps:**
- Use descriptive chapter titles that include topic keywords
- Each chapter becomes an individually indexable segment
- Example: "03:45 — How to Check If AI Crawlers Can Access Your Site" is better than "03:45 — Checking Access"

### Do's

- Create content that answers specific questions your audience asks
- Include specific data, numbers, and examples in your spoken content
- Engage with comments — YouTube's algorithm and AI both value active community engagement
- Cross-link relevant videos to build topic clusters within your channel

### Don'ts

- Don't rely on text overlays alone — AI reads transcripts, not on-screen text
- Don't publish short, shallow videos just for volume. One thorough 15-minute video outperforms ten 2-minute clips.
- Don't neglect the description field. An empty description is a missed citation opportunity.

## 3. LinkedIn

LinkedIn is the primary citation source for business, industry, and career-related AI queries. Professional content published on LinkedIn carries authority for B2B topics that personal blogs often lack.

### Why AI Cites LinkedIn

- LinkedIn articles have permanent URLs that AI can index and cite
- Professional credibility signals (job title, company, connections) add authority context
- Industry analysis and original data shared on LinkedIn gets cited for business queries
- LinkedIn content is perceived as professional and vetted (vs. anonymous forums)

### Articles vs. Posts

This distinction is critical:
- **Articles** (LinkedIn's long-form publishing): Have permanent URLs, are indexable by search engines and AI, and persist indefinitely. These are citable.
- **Posts** (feed updates): Are ephemeral, not reliably indexable, and unlikely to be cited by AI. Good for engagement but not for building citation surface.

Invest in articles for AI citation. Use posts for engagement and driving traffic to articles.

### Strategy

- Publish long-form articles (1,000+ words) with original data, analysis, or frameworks
- Include specific statistics and data points — AI citations favor quantitative content
- Use clear, descriptive headings that match how professionals ask questions
- Comment substantively on industry discussions — your comments appear in AI context when the discussion is cited
- Build connections with industry voices — their engagement amplifies your content's visibility

### Do's

- Share original insights from your professional experience
- Reference specific data, case studies, or frameworks
- Engage genuinely with others' content — thoughtful comments build visibility
- Maintain a complete, keyword-rich profile (AI associates your profile info with your content)

### Don'ts

- Don't use LinkedIn as a broadcast channel for press releases
- Don't write vague motivational content — AI doesn't cite platitudes
- Don't neglect your profile. A sparse profile undermines the authority of your articles.

## 4. Wikipedia

Wikipedia has extremely high authority for AI citation. If your brand or topic has a Wikipedia article, it carries outsized weight in AI-generated answers. AI engines treat Wikipedia as a foundational knowledge source.

### Why AI Cites Wikipedia

- Wikipedia is one of the highest-authority domains on the internet
- AI models are extensively trained on Wikipedia content
- Wikipedia's neutrality policy and citation requirements make it a trusted synthesis source
- Factual claims from Wikipedia are cited with very high confidence by AI engines

### Rules — Read These Carefully

Wikipedia has strict conflict-of-interest policies. Violating them damages your reputation and wastes effort.

- **NEVER edit your own brand's article.** This is a conflict of interest (COI) violation. Edits from company IP addresses or accounts linked to the brand get reverted, and your company gets flagged.
- **NEVER pay someone to edit your article.** Paid editing without disclosure violates Wikipedia policy and can result in your article being deleted entirely.
- **DO contribute genuine expertise to RELATED topic articles.** If you're an expert in AI search, contribute to the Generative Engine Optimization article — not your company's article.
- **DO ensure your brand is cited in reliable secondary sources first.** Wikipedia requires independent, reliable sources for notability. No media coverage = no Wikipedia article.

### Strategy

The path to a Wikipedia article is indirect:

1. **Build notability.** Get covered in reliable secondary sources: major publications, industry reports, academic citations. Wikipedia editors use these sources to establish that your brand is notable enough for an article.
2. **Contribute to the ecosystem.** Edit related topic articles where you have genuine expertise. Build a Wikipedia editing history that demonstrates good faith.
3. **Let it happen naturally.** Once you have sufficient media coverage and industry recognition, Wikipedia editors may create an article about your brand on their own. This is the legitimate path.
4. **If you want to suggest an article:** Use Wikipedia's "Request an article" process or the Conflict of Interest noticeboard to transparently disclose your affiliation and suggest the topic.

### Do's

- Build an extensive track record of media coverage and independent mentions
- Contribute to topic-area articles where you have domain knowledge
- Follow Wikipedia's Conflict of Interest guidelines precisely

### Don'ts

- Don't edit your own article — full stop
- Don't create an article about your own brand
- Don't use sockpuppet accounts
- Don't add promotional language to any Wikipedia article

## 5. GitHub

GitHub is the critical platform for developer-focused products. AI engines heavily cite GitHub README files, documentation, and issue discussions when answering technical questions.

### Why AI Cites GitHub

- README files are structured documentation that AI engines can easily parse
- GitHub's popularity and trust signals make it a high-authority source for technical content
- Issue discussions and documentation pages contain problem-solution pairs that match AI query patterns
- Repositories with high star counts signal community trust

### Strategy

**README quality is everything:**
- Write comprehensive READMEs that explain concepts, not just installation commands
- Structure with clear headings: What it does, Why it exists, How to use it, API reference, Examples, FAQ
- Include code examples for common use cases — AI cites these in "how to" answers
- Add a "Frequently Asked Questions" section — these map directly to AI queries

**Documentation:**
- Maintain thorough documentation in /docs or a documentation site
- Cover common questions and troubleshooting scenarios
- Update documentation when features change — stale docs reduce citation quality

**Community participation:**
- Contribute to relevant open-source projects in your space
- Answer issues thoughtfully — issue discussions become AI-citable content
- Create useful tools, libraries, or utilities that others reference

### Do's

- Write READMEs as if they're the primary introduction to your product
- Include "Why" sections, not just "How" — explain the problem you solve
- Keep documentation current with versioned content

### Don'ts

- Don't treat the README as an afterthought with just installation commands
- Don't let issues pile up unanswered — active maintenance signals quality
- Don't neglect documentation for non-technical users if your product serves them

## 6. Stack Overflow and Industry Forums

Expert answers on Q&A platforms get cited by AI when it encounters technical questions. Stack Overflow is the highest-authority Q&A platform, but industry-specific forums (Hacker News, Dev.to, Shopify Community, WordPress.org forums) also carry weight for their respective domains.

### Why AI Cites These Platforms

- Q&A format directly matches AI query-response patterns
- Community voting surfaces the best answers — AI trusts community-validated content
- Expert answers with code examples and explanations are information-dense
- Stack Overflow's strict quality standards mean cited content is reliable

### Strategy

**Answer quality over quantity:**
- Write thorough, well-structured answers: state the solution, explain WHY it works, provide code examples, mention caveats
- Answer questions in your specific domain of expertise — targeted authority beats broad superficiality
- Structure answers with headings, code blocks, and numbered steps for extractability

**Building reputation:**
- Focus on 2-3 tags/topics where you have genuine expertise
- Answer new questions quickly — first thorough answer often becomes the accepted answer
- Edit and improve existing answers when they're outdated

### Do's

- Provide complete solutions with explanations, not just code snippets
- Explain the "why" behind your solution — AI values explanatory content
- Include links to relevant documentation (including yours) when they add value
- Update your answers when APIs or tools change

### Don'ts

- Don't post link-only answers — they get flagged and removed
- Don't promote your product unless it genuinely solves the asker's specific problem
- Don't copy-paste generic answers — each response should be tailored to the question

## 7. Industry Publications

Guest articles on respected industry publications extend your brand's citation surface area while simultaneously building traditional backlink authority. This is where SEO and GEO compound most directly.

### Why AI Cites Industry Publications

- Publications that rank for queries your audience searches are the same sources AI engines retrieve from
- Bylined articles establish your brand as a contributor to industry knowledge
- AI engines treat recurring contributors to respected publications as authoritative voices
- Evergreen content on publications gets cited long after publication date

### Strategy

**Target selection:**
- Identify publications that rank for keywords your audience searches
- Prioritize publications with high domain authority (DA/DR 50+)
- Check if the publication accepts guest contributions — many have contributor programs
- Start with mid-tier publications and work up to top-tier as you build a byline portfolio

**Content approach:**
- Prioritize evergreen content over time-sensitive news. AI engines cite reference material more than dated articles.
- Include data, frameworks, and specific recommendations — not vague thought leadership
- Create content that can stand on its own as a comprehensive resource on its subtopic
- Reference your original research, tools, or frameworks by name (branded mentions)

**Publication relationship:**
- Build an ongoing contributor relationship, not one-off placements
- Contribute consistently — monthly or quarterly articles build cumulative authority
- Engage with the publication's community (comments, social shares of other contributors' work)

### Do's

- Lead with data and unique insights — publications want original value, not rehashed content
- Include your brand name naturally where it adds context ("In our analysis at [Brand], we found...")
- Write for the publication's audience, not yours. Adapt tone and depth to their readership.
- Provide genuinely useful content that would merit publication even without the backlink

### Don'ts

- Don't submit thinly veiled product pitches as guest articles
- Don't recycle content that's already published on your own blog
- Don't neglect the publication's editorial guidelines — follow them precisely
- Don't write time-sensitive content that becomes stale quickly. "Q1 2026 trends" has a short citation window. "How to build X" persists for years.

## Platform Prioritization

Not every business needs presence on all seven platforms. Prioritize based on your audience and industry:

| Business Type | Priority Platforms | Secondary Platforms |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Developer tools | GitHub, Stack Overflow, Reddit, Hacker News | YouTube, Industry publications |
| B2B SaaS | LinkedIn, Reddit, Industry publications | YouTube, GitHub (if technical) |
| E-commerce | YouTube, Reddit, Industry forums | LinkedIn, Industry publications |
| Agency/consultancy | LinkedIn, Industry publications, Reddit | YouTube, Stack Overflow (if technical) |
| Consumer product | Reddit, YouTube, Industry publications | LinkedIn, Wikipedia (if notable) |

Start with 2-3 platforms. Build genuine presence there before expanding. Spreading effort across all seven simultaneously produces mediocre results everywhere. Pick the platforms where your audience already asks questions and where you can contribute genuine expertise.
