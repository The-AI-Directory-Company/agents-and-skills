# Brand Mentions

Unlinked mentions of your brand contribute to perceived authority for both search engines and AI systems. Google's entity recognition treats brand mentions as implicit endorsements — they don't need a hyperlink to register. AI engines weigh brand mentions across diverse sources when deciding what to cite. If your brand name appears alongside topic-relevant terms in enough independent sources, AI models learn the association.

---

## Monitoring Setup

You can't convert mentions you don't know about. Set up monitoring before anything else.

### Free Tier

- **Google Alerts:** Set alerts for your brand name (in exact quotes), product names, and founder/CEO names. Use "All results" not just "Best results" — the "Best results" filter misses most mentions. Create separate alerts for each term.
- **Manual search cadence:** Weekly, search `"brand name" -site:yourdomain.com` on Google. Check Reddit search, Twitter/X search, and Hacker News search separately. These surfaces don't always appear in Google results.
- **Social platform search:** Each platform has its own search. Reddit's search, Twitter/X's search, and LinkedIn's search all find mentions that Google Alerts misses.

### Paid Tier

- **Ahrefs Alerts:** Web mention alerts and backlink alerts (new and lost). More reliable than Google Alerts for catching mentions on smaller sites. ~$99/mo as part of Ahrefs subscription.
- **Mention.com:** Dedicated mention monitoring across web, social, forums, news. Real-time alerts. From ~$29/mo.
- **Brand24:** Similar to Mention.com. Social listening with sentiment analysis. From ~$79/mo.

### Monitoring Frequency

- **High-velocity brands** (post daily, active in communities): Daily review
- **Most businesses:** Weekly review minimum
- **Low-activity brands:** Biweekly, but don't let it slip further

## Mention-to-Link Conversion

Unlinked mentions are free link opportunities. Someone already wrote about you — they just didn't link. Your job is to make it easy for them.

### Workflow

1. **Find unlinked mentions.** Search: `"brand name" -site:yourdomain.com`. Cross-reference results against your backlink profile to identify which mentions don't have links.
2. **Prioritize by site authority.** Target DA/DR >40 first. High-authority unlinked mentions are the highest-ROI conversion targets you have.
3. **Find the author's contact.** Check the article byline, then look up the author on LinkedIn or Twitter/X. If no author is listed, use the site's contact page.
4. **Send a brief, friendly request.**
5. **Track and follow up once** after 5 business days if no response.

### Conversion Email Template

```
Subject: Quick link request — [Article Title]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for mentioning [Brand] in "[Article Title]." We noticed there's no link
to our site for readers who want to learn more.

Would you mind adding a link to [specific URL — not just your homepage]? Happy
to reciprocate — our audience would find your [specific piece of their content]
really useful, and we'd be glad to share it.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Conversion Rate

Expect 5-15% of outreach attempts to result in added links. Higher for:
- Mentions on sites where you have existing relationships
- Mentions in recently published content (authors still remember writing it)
- Mentions where adding a link genuinely helps their readers

## Building Mentionable Authority

The best way to earn mentions is to create things worth mentioning by name. Generic advice gets attributed to nobody. Named frameworks get cited everywhere.

### Create Named Methodologies

Develop a branded framework, score, or methodology that others reference by name:

- **"[Brand] Score"** — A scoring system for something in your domain. People cite "according to [Brand]'s Score."
- **"[Brand] Framework"** — A step-by-step methodology. Practitioners reference "using the [Brand] Framework."
- **"[Brand] Index"** — A regular benchmark or measurement. Media outlets cite "the latest [Brand] Index shows..."

The name is the moat. If your methodology has a distinctive name, every mention is a brand mention.

### Publish Quotable Statistics

Create statistics people want to cite. This means:
- Running original surveys or analysis ("We analyzed 10,000 sites and found...")
- Publishing the results with branded attribution ("According to [Brand]'s 2026 report...")
- Making the statistics specific and surprising (specific numbers get cited; vague trends don't)

### Participate in Industry Surveys

When industry publications run "State of X" surveys or roundups, participate. Your brand appears alongside competitors, establishing category association. AI engines learn entity relationships from these co-occurrences.

### Contribute Expert Commentary

Offer named, attributed expert commentary to publications. Every quote is a brand mention:
- "[Your Name], [Title] at [Brand], says: '[quotable insight]'"
- This creates entity associations: your brand + your domain topic + expert authority

## How AI Engines Process Brand Entity Signals

Understanding the mechanism helps you optimize for it:

### Entity Recognition

AI models learn about entities (brands, products, people) from their training data and retrieval sources. Your brand becomes a recognized entity when:
- It appears across multiple independent sources (not just your own site)
- It co-occurs with topic-relevant terms consistently ("Acme Analytics" + "data visualization" across 50+ sources)
- It appears in authoritative contexts (major publications, Wikipedia, respected industry sites)

### Mention Frequency as Authority Signal

AI engines don't just count mentions — they weigh them by source authority and context relevance. But volume matters too. A brand mentioned in 50 articles without links still registers as more authoritative than a brand mentioned in 5 articles with links. Both signals matter; don't ignore unlinked mentions.

### Co-Occurrence Patterns

When your brand consistently appears alongside specific topic terms across diverse sources, AI models learn the association. This is how AI decides which brands to cite for which queries.

To build this:
- Ensure your brand name appears in content about your core topic on multiple platforms
- Get mentioned in comparison articles, roundups, and "best of" lists for your category
- Contribute to discussions where your topic is the focus (Reddit threads, forum posts, LinkedIn articles)

The goal: when an AI encounters a query about your space, your brand should already be strongly associated with that topic in its model.

## Tracking Mention Impact

Measure mentions alongside your other authority signals:

| Metric | Tool | Cadence |
|--------|------|---------|
| Total mentions (web) | Google Alerts + manual search | Weekly |
| Unlinked mention count | Cross-reference backlink profile | Monthly |
| Mention sentiment | Manual classification or Brand24 | Monthly |
| Conversion rate (mention to link) | Outreach tracking spreadsheet | Monthly |
| AI citation rate | Manual AI testing or Cairrot/AIclicks | Monthly |

The connection between mention growth and AI citation improvement typically lags 2-4 months. AI models and retrieval indexes update on their own schedules. Keep building mentions consistently and measure citation changes over quarters, not weeks.
