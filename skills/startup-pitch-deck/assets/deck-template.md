# Pitch Deck Template -- All 12 Slides

Copy-paste each block into your slide tool. Respect the word/character budgets -- slides are visual, not documents. If you cannot say it in the budget, move the detail to the appendix.

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Budget:** 20 words max

```
[Company Name]
[What you do in 10 words or fewer]

[Presenter Name], [Title]
Raising $[Amount] — [Stage] Round
[Month Year]
```

---

## Slide 2: Problem

**Budget:** 40 words max + 1 data point

```
[WHO] struggles with [PROBLEM].

Today they [STATUS QUO SOLUTION], which costs them
[QUANTIFIED PAIN: hours/week, dollars/year, error rate].

[X]% of [AUDIENCE] report [PROBLEM METRIC].
— [Source]
```

Rules:
- Name a specific persona, not "everyone"
- Quantify the pain with a number the audience can feel
- Cite the data point

---

## Slide 3: Solution

**Budget:** 30 words + 1 visual (screenshot or diagram)

```
[PRODUCT NAME] [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE].

Unlike [status quo], [PRODUCT] [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR],
so [TARGET USER] can [OUTCOME].

[Screenshot or product diagram showing the outcome, not a feature list]
```

Rules:
- Show the outcome, not the feature list
- One screenshot or diagram -- not three
- No jargon

---

## Slide 4: Market Size

**Budget:** 25 words + TAM/SAM/SOM visual

```
TAM: $[X]B — [Definition: everyone who could buy]
       [Source]

SAM: $[X]B — [Definition: segment you can reach now]
       [Source]

SOM: $[X]M — [Definition: realistic capture in 3-5 years]
       [Methodology: bottom-up calculation]
```

Rules:
- Build bottom-up, not top-down
- Every number needs a cited source
- "We estimated" is not a source
- SOM must be defensible with unit-level math

---

## Slide 5: Business Model

**Budget:** 35 words + unit economics table

```
Revenue model: [Subscription / Usage / Marketplace / Licensing]

[VALUE METRIC]: $[X] per [seat / transaction / unit] per [month / year]

| Metric     | Current   | Target (12mo) |
|------------|-----------|---------------|
| CAC        | $[X]      | $[X]          |
| LTV        | $[X]      | $[X]          |
| LTV:CAC    | [X]x      | [X]x          |
| Gross Margin | [X]%    | [X]%          |
```

Rules:
- If pre-revenue, show planned pricing with comparable benchmarks
- Use gross-margin-based LTV, not revenue-based

---

## Slide 6: Traction

**Budget:** 15 words + 1 chart

```
$[X]K MRR — growing [X]% month-over-month
[X] paying customers including [Notable Name]

[CHART: MRR or user growth over time — minimum 6 data points]

Key milestones:
- [Date]: [Milestone]
- [Date]: [Milestone]
```

Rules:
- Chart must show trajectory, not a single data point
- Investors scan for slope -- steeper is better
- 2-3 specific numbers below the chart

---

## Slide 7: Go-to-Market

**Budget:** 35 words

```
Current channels:
- [Channel 1]: [CAC] — [Volume/month]
- [Channel 2]: [CAC] — [Volume/month]

Sales motion: [Self-serve / Inside sales / Enterprise / PLG]

Scaling plan:
1. [Channel to scale] — why: [evidence it works]
2. [Channel to scale] — why: [evidence it works]
```

Rules:
- Show what works today before describing what you plan to do
- Include CAC per channel, not just blended

---

## Slide 8: Competitive Landscape

**Budget:** 20 words + 2x2 matrix or comparison table

```
[2x2 MATRIX]
  Axis X: [Dimension that favors you, e.g., "Ease of use"]
  Axis Y: [Dimension that favors you, e.g., "Depth of analytics"]

  [Your product]: Top-right quadrant
  [Comp A]: [Position]
  [Comp B]: [Position]
  [Status quo]: Bottom-left

Why defensible: [One sentence — network effect, data moat, switching cost]
```

Rules:
- Choose axes where you win
- Include the status quo (spreadsheets, manual process)
- Never say "we have no competitors"

---

## Slide 9: Team

**Budget:** 40 words for 2-4 people

```
[Name], [Title]
[One line: what in their background makes them right for THIS problem]
[e.g., "Built the payments system at Stripe that processed $1B+"]

[Name], [Title]
[One line connecting background to the mission]

[Name], [Title]
[One line connecting background to the mission]

Advisors: [Notable name, affiliation] (if applicable)
```

Rules:
- Connect each bio to the company's specific problem
- "10 years of experience" is not a bio -- name the relevant accomplishment
- Skip generic titles; highlight what matters

---

## Slide 10: Financials

**Budget:** 20 words + projection table

```
| Metric          | Year 1    | Year 2    | Year 3    |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Revenue (ARR)   | $[X]      | $[X]      | $[X]      |
| Expenses        | $[X]      | $[X]      | $[X]      |
| Headcount       | [X]       | [X]       | [X]       |
| Net margin      | [X]%      | [X]%      | [X]%      |

Key assumptions: [Growth rate], [Conversion rate], [Churn rate]
Current burn: $[X]K/month | Runway: [X] months
```

Rules:
- State assumptions explicitly -- do not hide them
- 3-year projection maximum
- Include current burn rate and runway

---

## Slide 11: The Ask

**Budget:** 30 words

```
Raising $[AMOUNT] at [STAGE]

Use of funds:
  [X]% Product & Engineering
  [X]% Go-to-Market
  [X]% Operations

This capital gets us to:
- [Milestone 1 with timeline, e.g., "$1M ARR in 18 months"]
- [Milestone 2 with timeline]
```

Rules:
- State the amount, use of funds, and milestones
- Milestones should be specific and time-bound
- "Raising $3M seed to reach $1M ARR in 18 months" -- one sentence

---

## Slide 12: Appendix

**Budget:** Unlimited (these are backup slides for Q&A)

Prepare slides for:

```
- Detailed financial model (monthly projections, scenario analysis)
- Product roadmap (next 12 months, prioritized)
- Customer testimonials or case studies (2-3 quotes with metrics)
- Technical architecture (if relevant to defensibility)
- Detailed competitive comparison table
- Cohort retention curves
- Sales pipeline breakdown
```

Rules:
- Do not present these unless asked
- Have them ready -- investors will ask
- Each appendix slide should stand alone with full context
