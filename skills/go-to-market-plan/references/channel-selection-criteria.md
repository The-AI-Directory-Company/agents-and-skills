# Channel Selection Criteria

Channels are not interchangeable. The right channel depends on who you are selling to, how they buy, and what your team can actually execute. This reference maps segments to channels with expected costs and applicability.

---

## GTM Motion Types

Before selecting channels, identify your GTM motion. Most companies have one primary motion and one secondary.

| Motion | Description | Typical ACV | Sales Cycle | Primary Channels |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Developer-Led** | Developers adopt bottom-up, upgrade when they hit limits | $0-$5K | Days to weeks | Content, communities, open source, docs, Product Hunt |
| **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** | Self-serve signup, usage-based expansion | $1K-$25K | Weeks to months | In-product, content, SEO, paid ads, referral programs |
| **Sales-Led (Mid-Market)** | Inbound leads qualified and closed by sales | $25K-$150K | 1-4 months | Outbound, events, webinars, partnerships, content |
| **Enterprise** | Named accounts, multi-stakeholder deals | $150K+ | 4-12 months | ABM, field events, executive dinners, analyst relations |

## Segment-to-Channel Mapping

### Developer-Led Motion

Target: Individual developers, small engineering teams, open-source users.

| Channel | Tactic | CAC Range | Time to Impact | Best For |
|---------|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Technical content | Blog posts, tutorials, comparison guides | $50-$200 per signup | 2-6 months | Awareness, SEO long-tail |
| Developer communities | Hacker News, Reddit, Discord, Stack Overflow | $0-$50 (time cost) | 1-4 weeks | Early traction, feedback |
| Open-source presence | GitHub, contributing to ecosystem tools | $0 (eng time) | 3-12 months | Trust, bottom-up adoption |
| Documentation | Docs site, quickstart guides, API references | $0 (eng time) | Ongoing | Activation, retention |
| Product Hunt | Coordinated launch day | $1K-$5K (prep cost) | 1 day spike | Launch awareness |
| Conference talks | DevRel at relevant conferences | $3K-$10K per event | 1-3 months | Credibility, networking |

**What does not work:** Cold outbound to developers. Gated content. Enterprise sales tactics. Developers will ignore or actively resent these.

---

### Product-Led Growth Motion

Target: Team leads, managers, and individual contributors who self-serve and expand within their organization.

| Channel | Tactic | CAC Range | Time to Impact | Best For |
|---------|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| SEO / content | Problem-focused blog posts, comparison pages, templates | $100-$400 per signup | 3-6 months | Sustained inbound volume |
| Paid search | Google Ads on high-intent keywords (competitor + category terms) | $200-$800 per trial | 1-2 weeks | Immediate volume, testing |
| Paid social | LinkedIn/Meta ads targeting job titles and firmographics | $300-$1,000 per trial | 2-4 weeks | Top-of-funnel, retargeting |
| In-product referral | Invite teammates, share reports, collaborative features | $20-$80 per referred user | Ongoing | Organic expansion |
| Email nurture | Onboarding sequences, usage-based upgrade nudges | $10-$50 per conversion | 1-4 weeks | Activation, free-to-paid |
| Review sites | G2, Capterra, TrustRadius — reviews and sponsored listings | $150-$500 per lead | 1-3 months | Mid-funnel, buyer validation |

**What does not work:** Relying solely on in-product virality without a content engine. Most PLG products need content to drive the top of funnel — virality alone rarely scales fast enough.

---

### Sales-Led (Mid-Market) Motion

Target: Directors, VPs, and functional leaders at companies with 200-2,000 employees.

| Channel | Tactic | CAC Range | Time to Impact | Best For |
|---------|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Outbound email/calls | Targeted sequences to ICP accounts | $500-$2,000 per SQL | 2-8 weeks | Pipeline generation |
| Webinars | Co-hosted with partners or customers | $200-$600 per registrant | 2-4 weeks | Mid-funnel education |
| Industry events | Booths, speaking slots, sponsored sessions | $3K-$15K per event | 1-3 months | Pipeline, brand awareness |
| Partner/channel | Resellers, consultants, technology partners | 20-30% of deal value | 3-6 months | Reach, credibility |
| Content marketing | Case studies, ROI calculators, industry reports | $300-$800 per MQL | 2-6 months | Inbound pipeline |
| LinkedIn organic | Founder/exec thought leadership, employee advocacy | $0 (time cost) | 1-3 months | Brand, inbound leads |

**What does not work:** Spray-and-pray outbound to massive lists. Sending 10,000 emails per month with a generic pitch burns your domain reputation and generates pipeline that does not close.

---

### Enterprise Motion

Target: C-level, SVPs, and procurement teams at companies with 2,000+ employees.

| Channel | Tactic | CAC Range | Time to Impact | Best For |
|---------|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Account-Based Marketing (ABM) | Personalized campaigns to named accounts | $5K-$30K per account | 3-9 months | Pipeline in target accounts |
| Field events | Executive dinners, private roundtables, advisory boards | $10K-$50K per event | 1-6 months | Relationship building |
| Analyst relations | Gartner, Forrester briefings and evaluations | $50K-$200K/year | 6-18 months | Credibility, shortlist inclusion |
| Executive referrals | Customer executives introducing peers | $0 (relationship cost) | 2-8 weeks | Highest-quality pipeline |
| Direct sales | Named AEs with territory plans | $15K-$40K per deal (fully loaded) | 4-12 months | Closing large deals |
| Strategic partnerships | System integrators, consulting firms | 15-25% of deal value | 6-12 months | Access to budget holders |

**What does not work:** Product Hunt launches. Self-serve trials without white-glove onboarding. Bottom-up adoption without executive sponsorship. Enterprise deals require top-down buy-in.

## Channel Prioritization Framework

When choosing channels, score each on these five criteria (1-5 scale):

| Criteria | Question | Score 1 (Low) | Score 5 (High) |
|----------|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Audience fit** | Does our target segment actually use this channel? | Segment rarely present | Segment's primary channel |
| **CAC viability** | Can we acquire customers profitably at this CAC? | CAC > LTV | CAC < 25% of first-year ACV |
| **Time to impact** | How quickly can we generate pipeline? | 6+ months | Under 4 weeks |
| **Team capability** | Do we have people who can execute this well? | Need to hire | Team has proven experience |
| **Scalability** | Can this channel grow with us? | Caps out quickly | Linear or compounding returns |

**Rules:**
- Pick 2-3 channels maximum for launch. Add more only after the first channels are working.
- Any channel scoring below 3 on "Audience fit" is disqualified regardless of other scores.
- Any channel scoring below 2 on "Team capability" requires a hiring plan before committing.
- Total budget across all channels should not exceed what you can sustain for 6 months — channels need time to work.

## Common Channel Mistakes

1. **Picking channels you like instead of channels your buyer uses.** The founding team loves Hacker News, but the buyer is a VP of Operations who reads industry newsletters. Go where the buyer is.

2. **Spreading budget across too many channels.** Three channels done well beats eight channels done poorly. A 2-person marketing team cannot run outbound, events, paid ads, content, partnerships, webinars, SEO, and social simultaneously.

3. **Expecting instant results from compounding channels.** Content marketing, SEO, and community take 3-6 months to produce meaningful pipeline. Budget for the ramp-up period or choose faster channels for launch.

4. **Ignoring CAC by channel.** Track CAC per channel, not blended. A $200 blended CAC might hide a $50 content CAC subsidizing a $2,000 paid ads CAC that will never be profitable.

5. **Confusing awareness with pipeline.** Social media impressions are not pipeline. Every channel needs a path to a measurable business outcome — signup, demo request, or qualified lead.
