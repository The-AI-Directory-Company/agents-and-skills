# Topic Cluster Architecture

How to organize content into interconnected clusters that build topical authority for search engines and provide AI engines with a coherent knowledge graph.

---

## What a topic cluster is

A topic cluster is a group of related content pieces with a defined structure:

- **One pillar page** — comprehensive overview of the core topic (2,000-4,000 words). Covers breadth. Targets the head keyword.
- **5-10 supporting articles** — focused deep dives on subtopics (800-2,000 words each). Cover depth. Target long-tail keyword variants.
- **Internal links** connecting them — every support links to the pillar, the pillar links to every support, and supports interlink where relevant.

This is not a suggestion. Isolated articles on random topics do not build topical authority. One well-structured cluster of 8 interlinked articles outperforms 20 disconnected posts — for both SEO rankings and GEO citation likelihood.

## Why clusters work

### For search engines

Google evaluates topical depth. A site with 1 article on "invoice automation" competes against sites with 8 interlinked articles covering every angle. The cluster signals: "This site covers invoice automation comprehensively." That signal translates into higher rankings across the entire cluster, not just the pillar page.

Internal links distribute authority. Every backlink to any page in the cluster flows authority to every other page through internal links. One strong backlink to a supporting article benefits the pillar page and all other supports.

### For AI engines

AI engines build entity graphs. When they crawl your cluster, they see interconnected content on a single topic — each piece adding a different dimension. This makes your site a preferred citation source because the AI can extract multiple relevant passages from your domain for different sub-queries.

A standalone article might be cited once. A complete cluster can be cited across dozens of different queries because each supporting article addresses a different question.

## Pillar vs supporting page sizing

| Aspect | Pillar page | Supporting article |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Scope** | Entire topic at overview level | One subtopic in depth |
| **Word count** | 2,000-4,000 words | 800-2,000 words |
| **Keyword target** | Head term ("invoice automation") | Long-tail variant ("invoice automation for small businesses") |
| **Depth** | Broad — covers all subtopics at surface level | Deep — covers one subtopic thoroughly |
| **Internal links out** | Links to every supporting article | Links to pillar + 2-3 related supports |
| **Update frequency** | Every 3-6 months | When topic changes or data ages |

**The pillar should not go deep on subtopics.** If you find yourself writing 800 words on a subsection of your pillar, that subsection is a supporting article. The pillar summarizes each subtopic in 150-300 words and links to the support for the full treatment.

## Internal link topology

The linking structure is critical. Follow this pattern exactly:

```
Every supporting article → pillar page (always)
Pillar page → every supporting article (always)
Supporting article A → Supporting article B (when topically relevant)
```

**Link placement rules:**
- Support-to-pillar links: place in the body content where the pillar topic is naturally mentioned, AND in a "Related reading" section at the bottom. At minimum, 1 contextual link + 1 navigation link per support.
- Pillar-to-support links: place in the relevant section of the pillar. When you summarize "Invoice Automation for Small Businesses" in 200 words, link to the full supporting article. Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here."
- Support-to-support links: link when one support references a concept covered in another. "For details on calculating ROI, see our [Invoice Automation ROI Calculator Guide]." Do not force links where they don't fit.

## Publishing sequence

Order matters. Follow this sequence:

1. **Publish the pillar page first.** It establishes the hub. Even without supports, it starts building topical signals.
2. **Publish 1 supporting article per week** (or per your cadence). After each: add a link from the new support to the pillar, and add a link from the pillar to the new support.
3. **After 3-4 supports are live: interlink pass.** Go through every published piece and add support-to-support links where relevant. Update the pillar to reference new supports added since initial publication.
4. **After all supports are live: final review.** Verify every link works. Ensure the pillar references every support. Check that no support is orphaned.

Do not start a second cluster until the first is complete. Depth beats breadth. A half-built cluster delivers a fraction of the value.

## When to merge vs keep separate

If two potential supporting articles cover nearly identical subtopics and you estimate each would be under 500 words, merge them into one stronger piece. Two thin articles dilute authority. One comprehensive article concentrates it.

**Merge when:**
- Subtopics overlap by more than 50%
- Either article would be under 600 words
- Search results show both keywords ranking the same pages (Google treats them as one topic)

**Keep separate when:**
- Each subtopic has distinct search intent
- Each can support 800+ words of unique content
- Search results show different page types ranking for each keyword

## Cluster completion metrics

A cluster is "complete" when:

- [ ] Pillar page published and ranking for head term
- [ ] All planned supporting articles published (minimum 5)
- [ ] Every support links to the pillar
- [ ] Pillar links to every support
- [ ] Support-to-support links added where relevant
- [ ] No orphan pages (every page has at least 2 internal links pointing to it)
- [ ] At least 3 supporting articles ranking for their target long-tail keywords
- [ ] Pillar page position improved since supports were published

Track these metrics monthly. A complete cluster should show measurable ranking improvement within 2-3 months of completion.

## Worked examples

### Example 1: SaaS — Invoice Automation

```
[Pillar: Complete Guide to Invoice Automation]
  |-- [Support: How to Automate Invoice Data Extraction]
  |-- [Support: Invoice Automation vs Manual Processing: Cost Comparison]
  |-- [Support: 7 Common Invoice Automation Mistakes]
  |-- [Support: Invoice Automation for Small Businesses]
  |-- [Support: Advanced Invoice Matching Techniques]
  |-- [Support: How to Choose Invoice Automation Software]
  |-- [Support: Invoice Automation ROI Calculator Guide]
```

- Pillar targets: "invoice automation" (informational head term)
- Supports target long-tail variants by intent: informational ("how to"), commercial ("vs", "how to choose"), hybrid ("mistakes", "ROI calculator")
- Publishing order: pillar first, then "how to" supports (easiest wins), then commercial supports (higher conversion value)

### Example 2: E-commerce — Running Shoes

```
[Pillar: The Complete Running Shoe Guide: Find Your Perfect Pair]
  |-- [Support: Best Trail Running Shoes — Tested on 5 Terrains]
  |-- [Support: Road Running Shoes vs Trail Shoes: Which Do You Need?]
  |-- [Support: Running Shoes for Beginners: What to Buy First]
  |-- [Support: How to Prevent Running Injuries with Proper Footwear]
  |-- [Support: Nike vs Brooks vs ASICS: Running Shoe Brand Comparison]
  |-- [Support: When to Replace Your Running Shoes (Mileage Guide)]
```

- Pillar targets: "running shoe guide" (informational)
- Supports mix informational and commercial intent
- "Best Trail Running Shoes — Tested on 5 Terrains" includes original value (first-hand testing) for both E-E-A-T and GEO citation potential
- Brand comparison post targets high-CPC commercial keywords

- "Digital Estate Planning" targets a growing niche with low competition — quick win

## GEO considerations for topic clusters

AI engines follow internal links to build context about your expertise. A cluster tells the AI: "This site has comprehensive, interconnected knowledge about [topic]." This makes any individual page in the cluster more likely to be cited because the AI has already mapped your topical coverage.

To maximize GEO value from clusters:
- Use question-format H2s in supporting articles (they match AI queries)
- Include self-contained knowledge blocks in every section (extractable for citation)
- Add specific data and sources throughout (citation-worthy passages)
- Ensure the pillar page's opening 200 words directly answer the core question (TLDR-first pattern)

A complete cluster with GEO-optimized content becomes a citation magnet — the AI has multiple entry points into your content and multiple passages it can quote.
