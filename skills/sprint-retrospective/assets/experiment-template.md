# Retrospective Experiment Template

Use this template to convert prioritized retro themes into actionable experiments. Fill in every field — incomplete experiments do not get done.

---

## Experiment: [Number]

### Theme

*What problem area did the team identify?*

[Write the theme in one sentence. Reference specific observations from the retro, not vague categories.]

**Example:** "Stories pile up in QA during the last 2 days of the sprint, creating a testing bottleneck."
**Not this:** "QA is slow."

---

### Hypothesis

*What do you believe will happen if you make a specific change?*

**Format:** If we [specific change], then [expected outcome], measured by [metric].

[Write the hypothesis here.]

**Example:** "If we set a soft deadline of Wednesday EOD for moving stories to QA, then testing will spread across 3 days instead of 1, measured by the date each story enters the QA column."
**Not this:** "If we improve QA, things will be better."

---

### Experiment

*What exactly will the team do differently?*

[Describe the specific action. Be concrete enough that someone who missed the retro could follow this.]

**Example:** "During Sprint 15, stories not in QA by Thursday standup will be flagged. The facilitator will remind the team on Tuesday and Wednesday standups."
**Not this:** "We'll try to get things to QA earlier."

---

### Owner

*Who is the single person responsible for making sure this experiment runs?*

**Name:** [One person — not "the team"]

The owner does not have to do all the work. They are responsible for ensuring the experiment is executed and reporting results at the next retro.

---

### Duration

*How long will the experiment run before evaluating results?*

**Duration:** [1 sprint / 2 sprints / other]

Most experiments should run for 1-2 sprints. Shorter than 1 sprint does not produce enough data. Longer than 2 sprints delays feedback.

---

### Success Criteria

*How will the team know if this experiment worked?*

[Write measurable criteria. The team should be able to answer "yes" or "no" at the evaluation point.]

**Example:** "80% or more of stories enter QA by Wednesday of week 2, compared to the current baseline of ~30%."
**Not this:** "QA feels less rushed."

---

### Baseline (Current State)

*What is the current measurement before the experiment starts?*

[Record the current state so you can compare at the end.]

**Example:** "In Sprint 14, 2 of 7 stories (29%) entered QA before Thursday. Average QA time per story was 6 hours, compressed into 1.5 days."

---

### Evaluation Date

*When will the team review results?*

**Date:** [Specific date — typically the next retro]

---

## Field Instructions Summary

| Field | Required | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Theme | Yes | Grounds the experiment in a real observation |
| Hypothesis | Yes | Makes the expected cause-and-effect explicit and testable |
| Experiment | Yes | Defines the concrete action — removes ambiguity |
| Owner | Yes | Creates accountability — one person, not "the team" |
| Duration | Yes | Prevents experiments from running indefinitely |
| Success Criteria | Yes | Makes evaluation objective — "did it work?" has a clear answer |
| Baseline | Recommended | Enables before/after comparison |
| Evaluation Date | Recommended | Ensures follow-through at next retro |

---

*Copy this template for each experiment. Maximum 3 experiments per retrospective.*
