# Question Frameworks by Role

Different stakeholder roles care about different things. This reference provides role-specific question banks organized by the stakeholder's function and decision-making lens. Select the appropriate section before each interview and pick 6-10 questions to adapt to your context.

---

## Executive (C-Suite, VP, Director)

**What they care about:** Strategic alignment, ROI, timeline, risk to the business, competitive positioning, resource allocation.

**Lens:** "Will this move the needle on outcomes I am accountable for?"

### Discovery Questions

1. "What business outcome does this initiative need to deliver for you to consider it successful?"
2. "Where does this rank among your top 3-5 priorities this quarter? What would cause it to move up or down?"
3. "What is the cost of not doing this -- or doing it 6 months later?"
4. "Who are the key stakeholders whose buy-in is essential, and do we have it?"
5. "What competitive or market pressure is driving the urgency on this?"

### Constraint Questions

6. "What is the budget envelope? Is it fixed, or flexible if we can demonstrate ROI?"
7. "Are there board commitments, investor milestones, or regulatory deadlines that constrain the timeline?"
8. "What organizational changes (reorgs, hiring freezes, leadership transitions) could affect this initiative?"

### Priority Alignment Questions

9. "If we can only deliver three capabilities in the first release, which three would you choose?"
10. "What would you cut to free up resources for this if you had to?"
11. "How will you measure whether this was worth the investment? What does the 6-month retrospective look like?"

### Risk Questions

12. "What is the single biggest risk you see to this initiative?"
13. "Have we attempted something similar before? What happened, and what should we learn from it?"
14. "What would cause you to pull the plug on this?"

## Operator (Team Lead, Manager, Process Owner)

**What they care about:** Workflow impact, change management, team capacity, handoffs, edge cases, operational burden.

**Lens:** "How will this change what my team does every day?"

### Process Mapping Questions

1. "Walk me through the current workflow from start to finish. Where are the bottlenecks?"
2. "Where do handoffs happen between your team and others? What information gets lost or delayed?"
3. "Which steps in the current process are manual that you wish were automated?"
4. "What workarounds has your team built to deal with limitations of the current system?"

### Impact Assessment Questions

5. "If we change this process, how many people on your team are affected? What does their ramp-up look like?"
6. "What is the transition period going to look like? Can you run both systems in parallel, or is it a hard cutover?"
7. "What training or documentation would your team need to adopt this?"

### Edge Case Questions

8. "What happens when something goes wrong in the current process? Walk me through a recent example."
9. "Are there seasonal peaks, special cases, or exceptions that a new system needs to handle?"
10. "What is the most annoying thing about the current system that nobody has fixed because it's 'not a priority'?"

### Capacity Questions

11. "Does your team have bandwidth to participate in testing, feedback, or UAT? If not, what would need to change?"
12. "What is your team's biggest resource constraint right now -- people, tools, or time?"

## End User (Individual Contributor, Customer, Front-Line Staff)

**What they care about:** Usability, daily friction, task completion, reliability, whether anyone listens to their feedback.

**Lens:** "Will this make my day easier or harder?"

### Pain Point Discovery

1. "What is the most frustrating part of your day related to [process/tool]?"
2. "If you could change one thing about how this works, what would it be?"
3. "How much time do you spend per week on [specific task]? Does that feel reasonable?"
4. "When was the last time the system did not work the way you expected? What happened?"

### Workflow Questions

5. "Show me how you do [specific task] right now -- walk me through it step by step."
6. "What do you do when the system doesn't let you do what you need? What's your workaround?"
7. "Which tools or systems do you switch between to complete [task]? What information do you re-enter?"

### Satisfaction Questions

8. "On a scale of 1-5, how confident are you that the current system gives you accurate information?"
9. "When you have a problem, how do you get help? Is that working well?"
10. "Have you given feedback about this before? What happened with it?"

### Future State Questions

11. "If the new system worked perfectly, what would be different about your day?"
12. "What features or capabilities would you most want? Least want?"
13. "What is one thing we should absolutely not change?"

## Legal / Compliance

**What they care about:** Regulatory requirements, data handling, audit trails, liability, contractual obligations, privacy.

**Lens:** "What are the non-negotiable rules, and what happens if we break them?"

### Regulatory Questions

1. "What regulations apply to this data or process? (GDPR, HIPAA, SOX, PCI-DSS, industry-specific)"
2. "Are there upcoming regulatory changes we need to design for now?"
3. "What are the data residency requirements? Does data need to stay in specific jurisdictions?"

### Data Handling Questions

4. "What data classification levels are involved (public, internal, confidential, restricted)?"
5. "Who is authorized to access this data? How is access controlled and audited today?"
6. "What are the data retention requirements? When must data be deleted?"
7. "Are there consent requirements for collecting or processing this data?"

### Audit and Documentation Questions

8. "What audit trail requirements exist? What actions need to be logged and for how long?"
9. "Will this system be in scope for any upcoming audits (SOC 2, ISO 27001, regulatory exam)?"
10. "What documentation or evidence do we need to produce for compliance reviews?"

### Risk and Liability Questions

11. "What is the worst-case scenario if something goes wrong with this system from a legal perspective?"
12. "Are there contractual obligations with customers or vendors that constrain our options?"
13. "Do we need legal review before launch? What is the typical review timeline?"

## Finance (CFO, Controller, FP&A)

**What they care about:** Cost, ROI, budget impact, forecasting accuracy, revenue recognition, financial controls.

**Lens:** "What does this cost, what does it save, and how do we account for it?"

### Budget Questions

1. "What is the total cost of ownership -- build cost, run cost, and support cost over 3 years?"
2. "Does this replace an existing system? What is the current annual cost of the system being replaced?"
3. "Are there licensing, infrastructure, or headcount costs that are not in the initial estimate?"

### ROI Questions

4. "What is the expected revenue impact? Is it new revenue, retained revenue, or cost savings?"
5. "When do we expect to see financial returns? What is the payback period?"
6. "How will we measure the financial impact after launch? What metrics will you report to finance?"

### Forecasting Questions

7. "How does this initiative affect the current quarter's forecast? Next quarter's?"
8. "Are there capitalization vs. expense implications we need to plan for?"
9. "What happens to the financial model if the timeline slips by one quarter?"

### Controls Questions

10. "Are there financial controls (approval workflows, segregation of duties) that the new system must enforce?"
11. "How will this affect revenue recognition? Are there ASC 606 or IFRS 15 implications?"
12. "Does this create any new financial reporting obligations?"

### Vendor and Contract Questions

13. "What are the payment terms and exit clauses with the proposed vendor?"
14. "Are there volume commitments or minimum spend requirements we should be aware of?"
15. "Who owns the vendor relationship and contract renewal?"

## Selecting Questions for Your Interview

1. **Identify the stakeholder's primary role** from the categories above. If they span two roles (e.g., a VP who is also a daily user), pull questions from both sections.
2. **Pick 6-10 questions** that align with your interview goal (discovery, validation, priority alignment, or constraint mapping).
3. **Sequence from broad to specific.** Start with open discovery questions, then narrow to constraints and priorities.
4. **Adapt the language.** These questions are templates. Rewrite them using your project's terminology and the stakeholder's context so they feel natural, not formulaic.
5. **Leave room for follow-up.** Your best questions will emerge during the interview. Use these as a starting framework, not a rigid script.
